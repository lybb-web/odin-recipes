Alaskan Salmon Bake with Pecan Crunch Coating

Description

A delightful dish that features Alaskan salmon fillets coated with a crumby mixture of pecans and breadcrumbs. The combination of the rich, flaky salmon and the sweet, nutty crunch from the pecan coating creates a harmonius blend of flavors and textures. The salmon is baked to perfection, resulting in a crispy exterior and moisty interior. The dish may offer a balance of savory and sweet notes, making it a delicious and visually appealing meal. The use of high-quality Alaskan salmon adds a touch of freshness and a unique taste to teh overall culinary experience.

Ingredients

  • 3 Tablespoons Dijon Mustard
  • 3 Tablespoons Butter, Melted
  • 5 Teaspoons Honey
  • 1/2 Cup Fresh Bread Crumbs
  • 1/2 Cup Finely Chopped Pecans
  • 3 Teaspoons Chopped Fresh Parsley
  • 6 (4 Ounce) Fillets Salmon
  • Salt and Pepper to Taste
  • 6 Lemon Wedges

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F(200 degrees C). In a small bowl, mix together the mustard, butter, and honey. In another bowl, mix together the bread crumbs, pecan, and parsley.
  2. Season each salmon fillet with salt and pepper. Place on a lightly greased baking sheet. Brush with mustard-honey mixture. Cover the top of each fillet with bread crumb mixture.
  3. Bake fore 10 minutes per inch of thickness, measured at thickest part, or until salmon just flakes when tested with a fork. Serve garnished with lemon wedges.
